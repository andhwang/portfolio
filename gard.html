GARD


Peer-to-Peer Storage Solution

https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/368083232/1620601785?ref=458262&token=33549aaf


Final Project for my COGS 122 Interaction Design Startup class. In teams of 5, we came up with a business startup idea. Our's was a peer-to-peer drop storage idea that would let users conveniently store and pick up their belongings where they want and when they want by utilizing other users' existing storage space.

I did research (observations & interviews), storyboards, value flow model, service reenactments, A/B testing, and value proposition. I assisted in the crowdfunding campaign, competitive analysis, branding, and the final poster.








Problem


From observations and user interviews, we found that many of our peers had to inconveniently carry around their belongings, some more burdensome than others.

Approach


Allow users to store inconvenient items in a nearby location in a secure manner until needed. Retreiving items should be just as easy. Allow users with storage space to "rent" out such space without jeopardizing security and comfort.





User Needs


People that need to store something


  • Secure space
  • Close location of storage space for easy pick-up
  • Not too much $
  • Quick and easy drop-off and pick-up of item
  • Trusted storer
  • Guarantee of item integrity



Gards (people with extra storage space)


  • Guarentee of item legality
  • Item pick-up and drop-off to be on time
  • $ compensation for service





Storyboards


I then made storyboards detailing the user scenarios to present how our idea would solve the problems present. We sent out online surveys afterwards to get a better sense of user base and to see how much they related to the storyboards.














Competitive Analysis


BagBnB




Currently only based in Europe. Caters specifically towards travelers.


Traditional Storage Units




Expensive and storage spaces are all uniform in size. Storage units are more suitable for bulkier items and a large quantity of items.




Value Flow Model


Since this was a startup design, I came up with a value flow model to figure out how this idea would generate revenue. I utilized the concept of a shared economy, since users were renting out space from other users.





Prototyping


Service Reenactments


We tested out the core of our idea through service reenactments. I, along with 2 other team members, came up with a testing plan and executed it. We did 3 service reenactments and followed up each service reenactment with questions to gauge how users felt and to see how feasible certain aspects of our idea were. Afterwards, we made changes to the task if necessary following user feedback. We used fellow students and co-workers for the testing, as we felt they might be more inclined to participate.

The three tasks were as followed:

1) Storer picked up user's gym bag at student union center and stores bag at storer's apartment for a period of 5 hours. Afterwards, the storer would bring the bag and meet the user in front of the gym (a short distance away) at a designated time.

2) User brings surfboard to storer's on-campus apartment (closest from campus to beach) in the morning before class. User picks up surfboard later in the afternoon (7 hours later) from storer's apartment.

3) User goes to off-campus apartment to drop off luggage bag in the afternoon. User will come back the next day at night to pick up the bag from the storer's apartment.<

Results & Redesign Implications


From the first service encounter, we learned that people felt comfortable storing other people's personal items as long as the item was not too large or cumbersome. In addition, users felt more comfortable with the service when they were able to see the storage space. We considered implementing a proof of storage space concept for potential storage spaces to be verified, so users could view pictures of the space beforehand. Another thing we learned was that asking storers to take on the role of delivery for drop-off/pick-up of items was too much to expect. Lastly, users felt comfortable using the service for items that were not too valuable and replaceable.

From the second service encounter, we learned that people are willing to part with valuable personal items if carrying them around is not worth the effort (surfboard, camera equipment, etc.). We learned that people were willing to pay a small sum (~$6) for the service. Another thing we learned was people are patient to a certain degree in waiting for a pick-up and for a drop-off. We decided a penalty for being late would keep people accountable. In addition, a rating system for users and storers was implemented to keep people honest.

From the third service encounter, we learned multi-day storage was perceived as less secure than same-day storage. We tinkered with the concept of item insurance. Also, adding personal expertise and experience storers have handling certain types of items was noted. An option to update pick-up time by checking with the storer was added due to changes in plans.
