JAMZ


Playlist-Centered Music App Redesign

Final Project for my COGS 187A Usability & Information Architecture class. In teams of 3, we redesigned a pre-existing music app with a prototype tool like Azure or Invision to center around 3 core Playlist features: Create, Manage, Sorting.

I did research (interviews & needfinding), competitive analysis, and heuristic evaluations. I assisted in information architecture and in the prototype redesign.









User Needs


  • Easily accessible features
  • Intuitive buttons for actions
  • Fluid processes
  • Feel in control




Competitive Analysis


Flow


I conducted heuristic evaluations on different music applications (Spotify, SoundCloud, Apple Music, YouTube Red, Amazon Music). I compared the user flow for different aspects of Creating, Managing, and Sorting Playlists.















We wanted to make creating a playlist to be accessible as soon as the user opened the playlist option. In addition, we wanted users to be able to group playlists by topic or mood. Making the button icons more intuitive was another thing we worked on.

We also wanted to make editing songs in a playlist easier, whether it be re-ordering them or adding/deleting songs.





Prototype Redesign


After competitive analysis, we met up to visualize the redesigned prototype. We wanted to make sure we observed design heuristics while incorporating changes into our redesign. Afterwards, we fleshed out the prototype screens on Invision.
