CHOWNOW


Simplifying Deciding Where to Eat

Final Project for my COGS 120 Interaction Design class. In teams of 3, we came up with a mobile web app. My teammates and I designed a mobile app that helps users decide on a place to eat via minigame.

I did research (interviews & observations), storyboards, wireframes, paper prototyping, and prototype testing. I assisted with the competitive analysis.









User Needs


  • People often struggle in picking a place to eat, usually in groups
  • Balancing people's preferences
  • Simple, yet effective manner of discerning between options




Storyboards


I made a storyboard to illustrate the problem we saw our users faced and how our idea would solve that issue. We wanted to show how our solution would take away the problem of having too many options to choose from and simplify the decision-making process.








Prototyping


Paper Prototyping


Next, a teammate and I made paper prototypes to flesh out the layout and flow of the app. We added a log that would help users keep track of places they had eaten at recently so that users could easily access them.








Wireframes


Afterwards, we made wireframes to get a better sense of how our app would look. We came up with the concept of having users pick between 2 options for several rounds so that the decision making process came down to simply comparing 2 options.





Functional Prototype


Next, I set out to make a functional prototype on Heroku. My teammates conducted A/B testing on the number of rounds to present to the user. We concluded that users want more than 3 options presented to them, but not more than 6. This was measured through by the number of clicks on the "No, Play Again" button.








Final Presentation


Lastly, we made a poster for the judges to look at while we pitched our product. We wanted to show our design process and how our app tackled the problem we saw many of our peers face in social situations. I did the layout of the app screens to show how our app worked. I wanted to show our solution was a simple step-by-step process.
